Welcome to Types of Computers

Types of Computers

Computers come in different shapes and sizes.

  1. Desktop Computer – A big computer used at homes, schools, and offices.
  2. Laptop – A small computer that can be carried anywhere.
  3. Tablet & Smartphone – Touchscreen devices that work like small computers.
  4. Supercomputer – A very powerful computer used in science and space research.

Input and Output Devices

Input Devices (Used to give instructions to the computer)

  • Keyboard – For typing letters and numbers.
  • Mouse – For clicking, dragging, and selecting things.
  • Microphone – For recording sound.
  • Touchscreen – Used in mobile phones and tablets.

Output Devices (Used to see or hear results from the computer)

  • Monitor – Shows pictures and videos.
  • Printer – Prints text and images on paper.
  • Speakers – Play sounds and music.

Basic Computer Operations

How to Start a Computer

  1. Press the power button on the CPU.
  2. Wait for the computer to turn on.
  3. The desktop screen will appear.

How to Use a Mouse

  • Click – Press the left mouse button once.
  • Double Click – Quickly press the left button twice to open something.
  • Right Click – Press the right button to see options.
  • Drag & Drop – Hold the left button and move the mouse to shift an object.

How to Shut Down a Computer

  1. Click on the Start Menu.
  2. Select Shut Down.
  3. Wait for the computer to turn off.

Introduction to Software

What is Software?

Software is a set of instructions that tells the computer what to do.

Types of Software

  1. System Software – Controls the computer (Example: Windows, macOS).
  2. Application Software – Programs used for different tasks (Examples: MS Paint, Google Chrome, Games).

Introduction to the Internet

What is the Internet?

The Internet is a large network that connects computers around the world.

Uses of the Internet

  • Learning – Watching educational videos.
  • Communication – Talking to friends and family.
  • Entertainment – Playing games and watching cartoons.
  • Shopping – Buying things online.

Internet Safety Rules

  • ✔ Never share personal information (name, address, phone number).
  • ✔ Use strong passwords to protect your accounts.
  • ✔ Do not talk to strangers online.
  • ✔ Ask an adult before using a new website.
